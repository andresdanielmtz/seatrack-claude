About Seatrack

Seatrack visualizes how marine debris concentrates and drifts across the world's five subtropical ocean gyres — plus smaller high-density clusters in enclosed seas and major river outflows (the Mediterranean, Bay of Bengal, Gulf & Caribbean, East China Sea and Arabian Sea).

About the shapes: accumulation zones are not circular — they're irregular, current-shaped regions, so Seatrack draws each one as an organic outline that the debris field actually fills.

About the data: there is no public feed of individual debris GPS positions. Seatrack models thousands of debris particles circulating within each zone (clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere, counter-clockwise in the Southern), updating continuously. Concentration estimates and locations are approximate, based on published research from NOAA and The Ocean Cleanup. The data layer is isolated so a live observational feed can be connected later.
